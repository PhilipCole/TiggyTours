Runswick bay to Kettleness
Date:
11 February 2023
Distance:
7.5 miles/12 km
Time:
3 hours 45 mins

A long hike down the coast to Kettleness.



Park at Runswick Bay, hike down the coast and up the cliff tops to Kettleness. Check those tide times!



Car park and down the hill to the beach.









Travel along the beach a few caves in the cliffs to the right.



Travel along the beach for half a mile, there's a path up to the top of the cliff. Some slippery rocks at the start.



Then stairs...



...stairs...



...and more stairs.



After a rest, take in the views at the top, here looking back at Runswick Bay.





East along the clifftops. Here with Kettleness on the horizon.





What was once an old train station, was a weekend away with the scouts for me a few times.





On our visits with the scouts we'd sometimes explore this derelict church, now looking less derelict and like it's being used.





After the little detour inland to Kettleness it was time to get back the way I'd came to the CLeveland way and head furtehr East along the clifftops.



At this sign I turned off the Cleveland Way and headed inland following a footpath.



Then a quiet road through Goldsborough.



More footpaths until you get to an old railway bridge.



With a view of the old railway route, this direction heading to Kettleness.



Just after the bridge you join the footpath back down to the beach.



One final view of the beach and bay before a short uphill climb to the car park and Tiggy.
