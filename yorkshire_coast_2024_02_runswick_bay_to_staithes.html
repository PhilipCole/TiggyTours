Runswick bay to Staithes
Date:
17 Feb 2024
Distance:
8 miles / 13 km
Time:
3 hours 45 mins

A long hike up the coast to Staithes.

Now and then when fancying a change of seaside scenery, Runswick Bay is the beach of choice. In the winter months its free to park too, so one February I decided to park and have, what turned out to be a very muddy hike up the coast to Staithes.

If you are visiting the area and fancy a quick trip onto the beach Check those tide times!

This is the route I took, an anti-clockwise loop starting from Bank Bottom Car Park. Heading North and following the coast along the clifftops until I pased through Staithes then its a left turn inland fora couple of miles until heading back to Runswick Bay.



Leaving Tiggy at the car park, this is the closest this hike gets to Runswick Bay beach.



Follow the road uphill to Runswick Bank Top, taking a right turn between two buildings. Make sure you are the correct, left, side of the hedge, by the Runswick Bay Hotel car park. The Cleveland Way signpost is a little confusing and can seem to point you down the other side of the hedge and down someones drive.

Turn left, under what was once, I presume, a useful bridge. Now just a bridge in the middle of a field.



The route has you follow the A174 for a short while, I power-walked this bit as I never enjoy walking along roadsides. Then at Dalehouse bank, it's time for another bridge.



The rest of the route follows a path through some woods until you get to Newton Mulgrave. Then follow Newton Lane back to the A174, cross that and through a couple of fields to get you back to Runswick Bay.
